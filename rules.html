Tournament Process

1. Sign up for the tournament you wish to play in using the links on our tournament pages. (You need a battlefy.com account and a valid game account in order to be able to sign up for a tournament).

2. Check-in to the tournament within the first hour leading up to it's start time.

3. Join our chatroom before the tournament starts. (Players risk disqualification if they do not join).

4. Once the tournament starts, and brackets are generated, find your match and click on it to get more information.

5. Message your opponent on the Battlefy match page, add them on Hearthstone, and send them a challenge. (If your opponent does not accept your friend/challenge request within the first 10 minutes of the match being posted, tell an admin in our chatroom and they will help to resolve the issue).

6. When you beat your opponent you are required to take a screenshot of the winning/victory screens in case of any dispute that may arise.

7. After completing a game with your opponent, report who won on the match page before starting the next game. Do this after every individual game so administration can clearly see that you are playing. (when your next match is posted you can play, you do not have to wait unless stated otherwise by an admin)

Tournament Format

Conquest Tournament Format

  • Each player must submit three unique classes prior to the event that they will use for the entirety of the event.
  • When a player wins a game, the deck used by the winning player cannot be used for the remainder of the match.
  • The losing player can keep the same deck used or switch to a different one of their choice.
  • Players will be told what classes their opponent has available, but they won't know which class is picked until both players have picked.
  • Players may edit decks between rounds of the tournament. For example, decks, not classes, can be changed between the 1st and the 2nd rounds, assuming you are not late to your next match. (Players are only allowed to have 1 playable deck for each of their 3 classes during the tournament and your opponent is allowed to ask for a screenshot of your deckslots, without leaving the challenge screen, to verify that you only have 1 playable deck per chosen class)
  • If a player is found to be using different classes then the ones they chose or have more then 1 playable deck per chosen class during the tournament they will be disqualified.

Best of 3 Conquest

  • Matches will be in a Best of 3 Format. The first player to win 2 games within the match is considered the winner of the match and advances.
  • A player must win one game with two of their three decks to win the match.

Best of 5 Conquest

  • Matches will be in a Best of 5 Format. The first player to win 3 games within the match is considered the winner of the match and advances.
  • A player must win one game with each of his three decks to win the match.

Fairness and Sportsmanship

  • All players must be fair and sportsmanlike when playing in any and all of our tournaments and risk not only disqualification but banishment from all of our future events and chat.
  • If anybody intentionally disrespects the sense of fair play and sportsmanship they will be removed and permanently banned from our events and chat.
  • Toxicity, profanity, and inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated and will result in a permanent ban.

Administrators (Admins)

  • Admins are in control of the tournament and they are in charge, if they ask for proof, explanations, or to spectate a game, etc. you must comply with their requests/instructions or you risk forfeiting the tournament.
  • Admin rulings are final and indisputable unless proper proof is supplied to overrule it.

Servers and Accounts

  • Players living in regions other than the tournament's designated region are allowed to play in the tournament, they just need to have an account on that tournament's server.
  • Players must fulfill all the tournament requirement and must have an active and valid battle.net account on the tournaments designated server. If a player does not fulfill all the tournament requirements or does not have a valid/active battle.net account linked they will be disqualified.

Brackets and Chatroom

  • Seeding for the brackets are randomly generated and are not decided by registration order.
  • Once the tournament has started the bracket can not be changed and players can no longer sign up/be added to the bracket or change their classes.

Classes and Decks

  • All players, before every tournament, are required to pick 3 classes to use throughout the duration of that tournament. Players are not allowed to change their classes during a tournament and will be disqualified if found doing so.
  • Every player is limited to 1 playable deck for each of their 3 classes. Players found to be using more then 1 playable deck per class will be disqualified. However, you are allowed to adjust and edit your decklists between rounds of the tournament, but not during a match.
  • During a tournament you can only have one deck made per class selected. Your opponent may request a screenshot of the challenge screen and if you have more than 3 playable decks, one per chosen class, you will receive disqualification. Failure to produce a screenshot will also result in disqualification.
  • If at any time you begin a game with a deck you're not allowed to use, you lose that game.
  • Sideboarding is not allowed and will result in disqualification. (Sideboarding means you may not change your deck/cards in your deck between the matches).

Friend Requests, Challenges, and No-Shows

  • If your match is posted you have 10 minutes to send a friend request to your opponent and challenge them or you risk disqualification.
  • If your opponent leaves a challenge at any time, even before you have played, you can choose to replay the match or disqualify your opponent provided that you have proof of the 'Challenge Canceled' screen. (If you leave a challenge due to disconnect or an in-game glitch you are required to get proof of it or risk disqualification)
  • If you have messaged your opponent on Battlefy and sent them a friend request on Battle.net and they do not respond after 10min contact an admin to resolve the issue.

Reporting, Proof, and Disputes

  • All players are required to report their matches on time, correctly, and truthfully or risk disqualification.
  • All players are required to take screenshots of all their individual games (victory screens) or risk disqualification.
  • If there is any discrepancy with your opponent involving his play, decks, etc. you are required to take screenshots as proof so that admins can give you a proper ruling.
  • If you believe your opponent is using multiple decks don't leave the challenge and ask them to take a screenshot of their deck slots without leaving the challenge to ensure that they are using 1 playable deck from each of their chosen classes.
  • If your match is disputed and your opponent has incorrectly reported a win you are required to contact an admin as soon as possible and be able to supply proof of your game wins. Lack of proof will result in disqualification.

Misclicks, Ties, and Glitches

  • In the event of a tie/draw where both participants drop to 0 health points in the same turn (both participants will see a "Defeat" as the end screen) the game will be remade with both participants using the very same deck again.
  • If you pick the wrong class, your opponent can grant a replay or take a win for that game.
  • If a player picks the wrong class and you do not get proof of it and/or play the game to the end, the game will count and the player will not get penalized.

Disconnect, Lag, and Glitches

  • If a player disconnects from a game it is their responsibility to join back and play it out or they lose that game, when a disconnect happens and you do not join back you have 5 minutes to re-challenge or you lose and forfeit the series.
  • If a player disconnects and you do not get proof of it, the game will be replayed and the player will not get penalized.
  • However, if you can prove with screenshots that you have imminent lethal you can be rewarded the win for that game.

Spectators, Streaming, and Recording

  • Spectators are allowed, however spectators are not allowed to spectate both players simultaneously.
  • Players are allowed to stream their matches throughout the tournament but HGL is not held responsible for any cheating, disconnects, etc that happen in result of streaming the event.
  • Recording your matches is also allowed but HGL is not held responsible for anything that happens in result to the recording of the event.
